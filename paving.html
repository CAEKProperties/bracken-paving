Residential

The Bracken team is dedicated to designing and building durable driveways. This commitment is backed with our 2 year warranty.

What is the difference between capping a driveway and creating a new driveway?

Capping (or an overlay) is when a 2" layer of asphalt is placed over the top of your existing driveway.

Whether you're starting with gravel, grass, or replacing an existing driveway, Bracken will install a new asphalt driveway. The process begins with grading for drainage: building the base for durability which is installing rock then compacting it. The final step is placing and compacting the asphalt with the appropriate depth.

Grading

Grade work is any work done to the existing ground before the paving of actual asphalt begins.

In most cases our graders:

  • Remove existing stone and/or driveway if necessary.
  • Remove grass, dirt, and debris in order to prepare for stone installation.
  • Ensure soil base is sufficient for proper water drainage.
  • Install a base of pug mix stone. (pug mix consists of rock, lime, and water. This product, once rolled, provides a very solid base for asphalt.)
  • When capping a driveway our grade crew will cut out and completely remove existing asphalt where the driveway meets concrete slabs and walkways. We do this to avoid creating trip hazards, spots that will hold water, and raveling from feathering the asphalt too thin.
